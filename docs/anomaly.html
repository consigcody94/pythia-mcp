04

So — a breakthrough?

The honest reading

No. The 125 GeV Higgs remains remarkably Standard-Model-like. The global fit prefers couplings within a few percent of unity, at barely ~1.5σ — a routine fluctuation. The channels scatter less than their error bars predict, and there are fewer 2σ outliers than random chance alone would produce. There is no signal of new physics in this database.

What is real and worth watching: the strongest tensions cluster in H→γγ associated production (WH/ZH/VBF→γγ), and the data leans gently toward CV > 1, CF < 1. None of it is significant today. A genuine breakthrough would be one of these patterns growing with luminosity instead of washing out.

That is exactly why live calculation matters: this page is a tension monitor. Refresh the database to Run 3, re-run the fit, and the moment a pull stops shrinking, you would see it here first — not in a press release.

The one door still ajar is the unseen: the Higgs may decay invisibly up to ~14% of the time. That is not an anomaly — it is the frontier where dark matter could touch the Higgs.

On the name

Lilith is a double meaning by design: an acronym for Light Likelihood Fit for the Higgs, and — as its authors note — the night-demon of myth, the one who refused to be lesser. A fitting patron for a tool that hunts what the Standard Model cannot see. (CERN keeps its own myth out front: a bronze Shiva Nataraja, the cosmic dance of creation and destruction — the dance of subatomic matter.) The iconography is a lens for thinking; the numbers above are the evidence.
